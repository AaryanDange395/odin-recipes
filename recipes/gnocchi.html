Gnocchi

Description

Gnocchi is a type of Italian pasta that is made from potatoes, flour, and eggs. The dough is formed into small dumplings that are boiled and then served with a sauce, olive oil, or melted butter and herbs. Gnocchi is a versatile dish that can be served as a main course or as a side dish to complement other dishes.

There are many variations of gnocchi, including those made with semolina flour, cheese, breadcrumbs, cornmeal, sweet potatoes, or rice flour. The dish is often flavored with herbs, vegetables, and other ingredients. Gnocchi is a popular dish in Italy and is enjoyed by people all over the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound potatoes (clean but not skinned/not new potatoes)
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 medium egg (room temperature)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil (40 grams)
  • 2 cloves large of garlic (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 5 leaves basil (chopped, or 1 teaspoon/3/4 gram dried)
  • 2 dashes hot pepper flakes (if desired)
  • 1 can pelati tomatoes (with sauce, 1 1/2 to 2 cups/400 grams, nothing else added in the tomatoes)
  • 1/2 cup water

Steps

  1. Boil the potatoes in a large pot of salted water until they are tender. Drain and let them cool enough to handle, then remove the skin.
  2. Pass the potatoes through a potato ricer or mash them.
  3. On a flat surface, mix together the flour and salt. Make a well in the middle and add the potatoes and egg. Mix together with your fingers to form a soft dough that should not stick to your fingers.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, cut small amounts of dough to form ropes and cut into 3/4 inch (2 cm) pieces. Then slide each piece on a fork and squeeze a little (but not too hard). Sprinkle with a little bit of flour and toss, so they donâ€™t stick together. Let the gnocchi rest for 20 minutes before cooking.
  5. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic, oregano, basil, and hot pepper flakes (if using) and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Add the pelati tomatoes and water and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Add the gnocchi to the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes, or until they float to the surface.
  8. Serve hot with grated Parmesan cheese.

Enjoy your dish!
